Website Pricing and Planning

How much does a small business website cost?

The cost of a small business website depends on the number of pages, design complexity, and features included. A simple informational website is usually more affordable than a site with booking tools, ecommerce features, custom branding, or advanced SEO support. Since 76% of consumers look at a business’s online presence before visiting in person, the goal should be to invest in a website that builds trust and helps customers take action.

What is included in a website design package?

A website design package often includes page layout design, mobile responsiveness, navigation setup, contact forms, and the main content sections needed for a business website. Search-friendly structure matters because Organic and paid search make up 68% of trackable website traffic. Depending on the project, a package may also include branding, custom feature setup, or post-launch maintenance.

What pages should a small business website have?

Many small business websites benefit from a homepage, an about page, service or product pages, a contact page, and sometimes an FAQ, gallery, reviews page, or location page. Reviews are especially useful for local businesses because 97% of consumers read reviews for local businesses. The right structure depends on what visitors need to know before taking action.

Website Process and Setup

How long does it take to build a website?

Website timelines vary depending on the size of the project, how quickly content is provided, and whether the website needs custom features. A smaller website can usually be completed faster, while a larger or more customized build may take longer. Revisions, image gathering, content writing, and integrations can all affect the timeline.

Do I need a domain name and hosting?

Yes. Every live website needs a domain name and hosting. The domain name is the address visitors type into their browser, while hosting stores the website files and makes them accessible online. Some businesses already have these in place, while others need help choosing and setting them up.

Can I redesign my existing website instead of starting over?

In many cases, yes. A redesign can improve the visual appearance, usability, structure, and clarity of an existing website without changing the core purpose of the business. Whether a redesign or full rebuild is better depends on the current condition of the site and how much flexibility is needed going forward.

Can a website be updated later?

Yes. Most websites can be updated over time to improve content, add pages, change images, refine the design, or introduce new features. That flexibility matters because businesses often grow, shift priorities, or need new content after launch.

SEO, Mobile Experience, and Maintenance

Can a website help with SEO?

Yes. A website’s structure, headings, page speed, mobile usability, metadata, and internal linking all affect how well it supports SEO. A well-built site makes it easier for search engines to understand the content and for visitors to navigate the pages. This matters because organic search alone accounts for 53% of trackable website traffic. Ongoing content and optimization also matter for long-term visibility.

Are websites designed to work on mobile devices?

Modern websites should be designed to work across phones, tablets, laptops, and desktops. Mobile responsiveness matters because search engines can use the mobile version of a site for indexing and ranking. A mobile-friendly site helps improve usability, credibility, and search performance.

Do websites need maintenance after launch?

Most websites benefit from ongoing maintenance. This may include software updates, security checks, content edits, backups, performance improvements, and support with future changes. Maintenance is especially important for CMS-based sites because WordPress is used by 41.9% of all websites monitored, and CMS websites often need updates to stay accurate, secure, and functional.

Choosing Features and Next Steps

What features can be added to a website?

Website features depend on the goals of the business. Common features include contact forms, galleries, booking tools, ecommerce functionality, testimonials, maps, service pages, blog sections, and customer inquiry tools. Strong CTAs help attract visitors and guide them toward action, so features like buttons, forms, and booking links should be easy to find.

How do I know what kind of website my business needs?

The right website depends on what the business offers, what customers need to know, and what action visitors should take. Some businesses need a simple online presence, while others need service pages, online booking, ordering, or lead-generation features.
